# Verifiable Sources for Model Grounding

This document lists established and authoritative sources for grounding model responses in verifiable math, data, and science. The model should prioritize these sources when fetching information to align with its operational protocol.

## 1. Major Scientific Databases and Indexes

These platforms are comprehensive and widely recognized for indexing and providing access to scientific literature and data across various fields.

- **Scopus:** A large abstract and citation database of peer-reviewed literature.
- **Web of Science (WoS):** A comprehensive research platform, covering a broad range of disciplines.
- **PubMed:** A free resource for searching and publishing scientific research in biomedicine and life sciences.

## 2. Open Access Repositories

These platforms are dedicated to making scientific output freely available, promoting open science.

- **arXiv:** An open-access archive for scholarly articles in physics, mathematics, computer science, and more.
- **Directory of Open Access Journals (DOAJ):** A multidisciplinary directory of high-quality, peer-reviewed open-access journals.
- **Zenodo:** A general-purpose open-access repository operated by CERN.
- **PubMed Central (PMC):** A free full-text archive of biomedical and life sciences journal literature.

## 3. Government Agencies

Many government bodies are primary sources for scientific data.

- **U.S. Geological Survey (USGS):** Provides reliable data on natural hazards, resources, and environmental health.
- **National Institutes of Health (NIH):** The U.S. medical research agency.
- **National Centers for Environmental Information (NCEI):** Holds one of the most significant archives of oceanic, atmospheric, and geophysical data.
- **Science.gov:** A gateway to U.S. government science information.
